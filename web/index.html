What Shuck does

Logs your trail

As you browse, Shuck records each page—URL, title, and time. Your visit history is always there to search or scroll through.

Snapshots on demand

When a page matters, hit “Snapshot this page” to save a screenshot and a hash. Tag it so you can find it later.

Export your record

Export a JSON dump or a clean HTML report with timestamps, links, and tags—handy for notes or sharing a trail with others.

Who uses Shuck

Researchers, journalists, analysts, and anyone who needs to retrace their steps or keep a clear record of what they’ve seen online.

  • Researchers & students
  • Journalists
  • Legal & compliance
  • Security & OSINT
  • Anyone who follows a lot of links

Get Shuck

Shuck is a Chrome extension. Install it once and use it from your toolbar. No account, no server—data stays on your machine.

  1. In Chrome, open chrome://extensions
  2. Turn on Developer mode (top right)
  3. Click Load unpacked
  4. Choose the extension folder inside this project

You only do this once. After that, the Shuck icon appears in your toolbar; click it to see your trail and snapshots. No web server or internet needed for the extension to work.

To view this page without a server: double‑click index.html or open it via File → Open in your browser.
